Complete Unity Portal Tutorial Part 1

22May

When I created my portal system, it was incredibly difficult to find good information. There’s actually some decent tutorials out there that explain the rendering side of things, which is relatively straightforward once you get your head around it, but the really difficult part is the physics and collisions. This tutorial series will start out with very basic portals that don’t do much, and progressively add complexity until we have a very complex but also very complete portal system.

Simple Recursive Portal Rendering

There’s two main ways to render portals. The first is render textures. Each portal renders to a render texture and the render texture is attached to a mesh (usually just a plane) in the world, which is then rendered by the main camera. The second method is stencil buffers, which I don’t know much about. I’ll be using render textures.

Note: If you’re thinking render textures have a big flaw in that the plane will get clipped away when the camera gets close, you’re right. Don’t worry, we’ll solve this too!

The first thing we’ll need is a place to start. Grab this project and load it up in Unity. This will be our starting point. It’s very simple, just a decent first person controller to let you move around. Nothing special. Feel free to hit Play and jump around until you get bored. You can change the sensitivity of the mouselook on the CameraContainer object. Right, let’s get to it.
